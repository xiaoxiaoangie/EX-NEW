## Translation Principles

1. **Industry Standard**: Use established payment and crypto industry terminology
2. **Consistency**: Same Chinese term = same English translation throughout
3. **Professional**: Avoid colloquial or casual language
4. **Clarity**: Clear and unambiguous for financial transactions
5. **Compliance**: Use terms that align with financial regulations

## Notes on Key Terms

### "承兑" Translation
- **Chosen**: Settlement
- **Why**: In financial context, "承兑" refers to the settlement/clearing of crypto-to-fiat transactions
- **Not Used**: Acceptance (too general), Exchange (different meaning)

### "对账单" Translation
- **Chosen**: Reconciliation Statement
- **Why**: Standard accounting term for matching transactions
- **Not Used**: Statement of Account (less specific), Account Statement (too general)

### "入金/出金" Translation
- **Chosen**: Deposit/Withdrawal
- **Why**: Standard banking terminology
- **Not Used**: Inflow/Outflow (too technical), In-payment/Out-payment (non-standard)

### "数币" Translation
- **Chosen**: Crypto
- **Why**: Industry-standard abbreviation for cryptocurrency
- **Not Used**: Digital Currency (too formal), Virtual Currency (outdated term)

### "法币" Translation
- **Chosen**: Fiat
- **Why**: Standard term in crypto industry for government-issued currency
- **Not Used**: Legal Currency (too literal), Traditional Currency (imprecise)
