## 2.2 Proof Points

> **The three roles** (every case is delivered jointly by all three):
>
> - **iPayLinks — Global licensed fiat clearing & settlement network**: global collection & payout, FX, cross-border clearing, and onshore RMB settlement.
> - **BlancBlock — Crypto clearing & liquidity network**: fiat ⇄ crypto (USDC/USDT) liquidity, market making, and on-chain real-time clearing.
> - **EurewaX — AI-driven full-stack tech platform**: clearing engine, real-time FX, unified ledger, intelligent risk control, orchestrating fund routing end to end (bookkeeping, reconciliation, FX rate check / FX lock, risk control).
>
> Each case follows: "**Payer → fund routing (fiat via iPayLinks / crypto via BlancBlock) → payee and settlement currency.**"

### Case 1 · Cross-Border B2B: Vietnamese buyer pays, Chinese seller collects

- **In plain terms**: the Vietnamese customer pays in local currency, and the Chinese seller receives RMB directly into a domestic account.
- **Scenario**: the Vietnamese buyer pays in local currency or crypto; the Chinese seller receives RMB into a domestic corporate account, with FX, compliance, and clearing handled end to end by the platform.
- **Pain points**: traditional wire transfers take 3–5 days, carry high fees, and face USD quota limits; FX volatility erodes the thin margins of trade.
- **Solution · Fund Flow**:
  1. The Vietnamese buyer pays locally: **fiat VND** or **crypto USDT**.
  2. Fiat side → **iPayLinks**: collect VND, convert **VND → USD → CNY**, completing cross-border clearing.
  3. Crypto side → **BlancBlock**: collect USDT, on-chain clearing (as needed **USDT → USD → CNY**).
  4. The Chinese seller receives **CNY** (onshore settlement into a corporate account, supporting trade declaration and tax rebate).
  5. **EurewaX** handles bookkeeping, accounting, FX rate check / FX lock, compliance & risk control, and reconciliation throughout.
- **Customer value**: same-day / next-day settlement, lower all-in cost, order-level auto reconciliation, full traceability.

### Case 2 ·Retails

- **In plain terms**: consumers pay however they like, and merchants receive in whatever currency they prefer — we handle the conversion and clearing in between.
- **Scenario**: consumers pay in their local method and currency (card / QR / local wallet / crypto), while merchants collect in the fiat or crypto currency of their choice (e.g. USD / CNH / CNY / USDC / USDT).
- **Pain points**: consumer payment methods are fragmented and cross-currency settlement is complex; merchants want to receive in a currency that suits them, but conversion and clearing are slow and costly.
- **Solution · Fund Flow**:
  1. The consumer pays with **any method** — card / local QR / local wallet / **crypto**.
  2. Fiat side → **iPayLinks**: collect and run FX + cross-border clearing.
  3. Crypto side → **BlancBlock**: collect crypto and run on-chain clearing.
  4. The merchant receives in **their chosen currency** — fiat (USD / CNH / CNY) or crypto (USDC / USDT).
  5. **EurewaX** orchestrates FX, risk control, and reconciliation throughout.
- **Customer value**: full coverage of consumer payment methods, free choice of merchant settlement currency (fiat or crypto), with fast, low-cost conversion and clearing.

### Case 3 · OTA Card-Issued Payouts: batch settlement to hotels / airlines

- **In plain terms**: issue "virtual cards" to travel platforms, pay hotels and airlines by card per order, with automatic reconciliation.
- **Scenario**: OTA platforms need to pay large numbers of hotels and airlines; the platform issues virtual cards by order / limit to complete payments and achieve automatic reconciliation.
- **Pain points**: upstream suppliers are fragmented; per-transaction payouts and reconciliation are costly, and fund control and security are challenging.
- **Solution · Fund Flow**:
  1. The OTA platform funds the settlement hub: **fiat via iPayLinks**, **crypto via BlancBlock**.
  2. **EurewaX** issues **virtual cards** by order / limit (fiat cards / crypto cards) and handles bookkeeping and limit control.
  3. Pay hotels / airlines by card; when FX is needed, **iPayLinks** handles fiat conversion and **BlancBlock** handles crypto clearing.
  4. **EurewaX** performs automatic reconciliation, risk control, and limit management throughout.
- **Customer value**: on-demand card issuing, controllable limits, automated reconciliation, secure and traceable funds.

### Case 4 · FX Lock Product: buyer pays USD, seller settles in CNY with factories

- **In plain terms**: the buyer pays in USD, but the seller pays its factories and logistics providers in CNY; the seller locks the USD-to-RMB rate when the order is signed, so its RMB cost stays fixed months later.
- **Scenario**: a US buyer purchases at a **USD price**, while the Chinese seller settles with its **factories / logistics providers in CNY**; FX volatility between signing and collection directly erodes the seller's margin.
- **Pain points**: the buyer's payment currency (USD) and the seller's cost currency (CNY) differ, and with a 30–60 day payment term, FX volatility creates uncertain margin risk for the seller.
- **Solution · Fund Flow**:
  1. The buyer orders at a **USD price**; on the signing date, the **seller locks the FX rate via EurewaX**, fixing the **USD → CNY** rate (30–60 days) to protect its CNY cost.
  2. Collection: the buyer pays USD (collected via **iPayLinks** in fiat; if paid in crypto, collected and cleared via **BlancBlock**).
  3. At maturity, convert at the locked rate: **iPayLinks** executes **USD → CNY**.
  4. The seller receives **CNY** and pays its **factories / logistics providers in CNY**; **EurewaX** handles bookkeeping, FX rate check, and risk control throughout.
- **Customer value**: the seller's RMB cost and margin are locked, FX volatility hedged, and payments to factories / logistics providers stay predictable.
