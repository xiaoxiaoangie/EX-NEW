Industry Pain Points — "Can't Open an Account"

The existential challenge for new MSOs / PSPs

🚫 Banks Refuse to Open Accounts

Banks classify MSOs/PSPs as high-risk clients (ML risk). Newly licensed institutions can barely pass bank compliance reviews. The process takes 3–6 months, severely delaying business launch.

🔗 Nesting Compliance Barrier

Other payment institutions (PSP/EMI) also refuse to open VAs for peers — regulators are concerned about "Nesting" (payment institution layering). Most PSPs' compliance policies explicitly prohibit opening accounts for other payment institutions.

💀 Business Cannot Launch

No bank account = cannot receive acquirer settlement funds = cannot operate acquiring/remittance business. The account issue is a make-or-break problem for payment institutions.

👥 Affected Groups

• Newly licensed MSOs: just obtained license, no banking relationships
• Recently acquired MSOs: banks distrust new shareholders
• Small PSPs/EMIs: insufficient scale
• Cross-border payment companies: involved in high-risk corridors

💡
Moolahgo's Unique Advantage: As a MAS-licensed Major Payment Institution, Moolahgo's compliance framework supports providing VA services to payment institutions without constituting Nesting. Already supports 14+ currencies covering core Southeast Asian markets.
